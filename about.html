About

We are 7 student who believe in NOT wasting food. We want to make booklets/ an app where the user can learn and get the full benefit of their food. Some of the information we cover is: what foods last longer than others, what parts of a plant you can or can’t eat, easy recipes on how to make food of what you already have at home and a lot of other tips and solutions!

We can do it!

Do you ever have food in the fridge that gets bad before you have time to eat it? You're not alone! A study from 2018 shows that the household sector accounts for more than half of all food waste in Norway and in total 1/3 of all food goes straight into the bin (matvett, 2019). That's a sad thought. It is not good for the environment nor is it economically appropriate. But do not be discouraged, because the same study showed that from 2015 - 2018 we had a decrease of 16,000 tons of food waste. This corresponds to a 12% decrease per capita. The food industry does its job and we want to help the population contribute their share. Therefore, we want to spread knowledge and information about how you as individuals can become better at using up the food we already have instead of just throwing it away. Because it is possible!

The UN Sustainable Development Goal 2

Zero Hunger

The UN's number 2 sustainability goal is to eradicate hunger, achieve food security and better nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture (UN Sustainability Goal, 2020).
The UN says that there is enough food for everyone in the world today, but due to the skewed distribution we have today, some in the world are starving, while others throw away food because they have too much. The goal is to be able to distribute the resources more fairly and take care of the food in a better way than we do today.
Sub-goal 2.4 states: "By 2030, ensure that there are sustainable food production systems, and introduce robust methods that increase productivity and production, that help maintain ecosystems, that strengthen the capacity to adapt to climate change, extreme weather, droughts, floods and other disasters, and that gradually improves the quality of land and soil. "

In Norway, we get more than enough food, and the problem is rather unhealthy diet and malnutrition. Luckily, there are plenty of healthy, tasty and simple tricks and tips for making both simple and advanced meals in a sustainable way. Read our tips on our solution page and get inspired.
